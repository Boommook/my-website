## Assignment 6: Langton’s Ants (Vants)

This assignment required me to recreate a simulation of vants using the seagulls library. Additionally, we had to implement three different vant behaviors. The first one I added was the basic vant behavior where the vants turn 90 degrees clockwise or counterclockwise depending on the vant type and if pheromones are present. These vants are colored red and blue. The next behavior type I added, was a faster vant, colored green. This vant moves twice the distance as a normal vant, leading to dotted patterns because the vant skips over every other cell.

Finally, I added a randomness factor that makes vants sometimes move in straight lines if no pheromones are present. Vants with such behavior will go straight until they find a pheromone, and then turn. These random straight lines build a spider web-like pattern, though the pattern differs from a spider web in that the pheromone lines do not overlap, and instead take sharp right turns.

Adding differing vant behaviors highlighted how small changes can lead to drastically different patterns. When I introduced the faster vants to the simulation, they caused gaps and fragmentation in the patterns. When I added the chance for vants to move straight, they produced long and simple paths. With many different behaviors, the simulation combined order and chaos.
